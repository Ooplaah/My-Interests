My Hobbies


With my ADHD I cycle through things like no other. I'll be interested in crocheting, then bracelet makeing, then book binding, then sewing, then wood working, then making sculptures, then beadwork, then wirework and so on and so forth. I'll circle back to it at some point, it's just no saying when, why or how. But in this page.I'll only discuss, crocheting, my fairy teacup project, foraging, and books.

Foraging


Foraging is really fun! Here are some of the books I have on it! I highly recommend: Sam Thayer's Field Guid to Edible Wild Plants. It's a little bit overwhelming to go through, but it's super informative. In my area, I usually forage things like mint, purple flowered raspberries, wild black berries, riverbank grapes, firelily shoots, dandelions and staghorn sumac. These are all pretty easy to identify so it's safe to eat. But please only consume things you are sure of and can clearly identify.

Books

I read books like a maniac. I recently have been listening to audiobooks going to and from school as well as while doing art. I highly recommend using the Public Library's app Libby or if you're more invested or want specific books, Audible.
This is the first book in the Empyrean series, with genres such as: Romance, Fantasy and Dragons (that's a genre in my heart). I listened to it for 15 hours straight, then woke up and listened to the last 5 hours. It was personally a great read and I ate it up. Only the second book is out at the moment so be warned if you choose to read.
