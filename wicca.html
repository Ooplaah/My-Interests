What is Wicca?


Wicca is a modern pagan religion that emphasizes harmony with nature, personal spirituality, and reverence for the divine. It's often associated with practices like ritual magick, herbalism, and worship of goddesses and gods, but typically at least follow mother Goddess and/or the Horned God. Wiccans typically celebrate seasonal festivals called Sabbats and conduct ceremonies known as rituals to honor their beliefs and connect with the spiritual forces they believe in!

Mother Goddess


In Wicca, the Mother Goddess is like the big cosmic mama figure. She's often seen as the embodiment of fertility, wisdom, and nurturing energy. Think of her as this divine force that's all about creation and the cycles of life. The Mother Goddess is celebrated in various forms depending on the tradition, like the Triple Goddess (Maiden, Mother, Crone), or specific goddesses from different mythologies like Isis, Diana, or Hecate. Basically, she's the ultimate divine feminine presence in Wiccan belief.
Personally I practice with the Triple Goddess, more so than any specific deity. The closest, I suppose entity, that I work with is my Spirit Guides, who I'll discuss later.

The Horned God


Wiccans often worship the Mother Goddess alongside the Horned God, her partner, representing the masculine side of things.
He's often depicted as this powerful figure with horns on his head, hence the name. Picture him as the embodiment of the wild, untamed energy of nature, kind of like the spirit of the forest or the hunt. He's associated with masculinity, virility, and the cycle of life, death, and rebirth. Some Wiccans see him as the god of the wild animals, the hunt, and even fertility.
Like the Mother Goddess, the Horned God has different forms and aspects depending on the tradition. Sometimes he's linked with specific gods from ancient mythologies, like Pan, Cernunnos, or even the Green Man. In Wiccan belief he's like the rugged, primal force of nature.

Spirit Guides


So, everyone has at least 1 spirit guide. Think of them as mentors from the other side, offering advice, protection, and wisdom when we need it most. They're believed to be entities—sometimes ancestors, angels, or even animals—that guide and support us throughout our lives. You can get signs from them, as well as communicate with them with numerous different methods, and generally ask for their help and guidance throughout your life. Some people also sense their presence, while others may receive messages or guidance through dreams or intuition.

Witchcraft


In Wicca, witchcraft is basically the magical side of things. It's all about tapping into the natural energies of the universe to create change or achieve certain goals. Wiccans often see magic as a way to work with the rhythms of nature and the divine forces around them.

Witchcraft in Wicca involves rituals, spells, and ceremonies that are designed to align with the cycles of the moon, the seasons, and other natural phenomena. It's about about focusing your intentions and energy to bring about the outcomes you desire.

Typically being wiccan and doing witchcraft, aka also making you a witch, come hand in hand. But that's not always the case. You can be wiccan without needing to do witchcraft, just as much as you can be a witch and not follow a faith; for witchcraft is a practice not a religion.

Also to add upon this, society typically thinks that witches are only female due to the multitude of medias portraying this narrative. But, being a witch is not prescribed to any gender, female, male, nonbinary, or anthying else in between.
